How Is the Competitive Scene?

The Super Smash Bros. series is very known for its competitive nature. Being a competitor at such a high level requires years and years of contstant practice, and in the case of Melee, there are even still veteran competitors of the game that have grinded out the game for over 20 years. It has been a competitive eSport since the start of the franchise in 1999, and has expanded tremendously over the years. Two of the main Smash games that get featured for their competitive-tailored gameplay are Melee and Ultimate. Melee is a very technical game, and requires constant precision with your inputs all of the time. It is also the fastest-paced Smash game in the series. Smash Ultimate on the other hand, is stil pretty fast-paced, but is less dependent on execution and more dependent on knowledge of your opponents, as since it is a bigger game with more characters, you have to be constantly aware of what is going on in the match to win at top level. Both of these games are very well regarded in the competitive scene, and they are treats to watch!
